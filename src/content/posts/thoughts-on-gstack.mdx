gstack sits in the tension between being overhyped and oversimplified, between "best thing since sliced bread" and "it's just a bunch of markdown files".

If you haven't heard about gstack, its a set of skills for your AI agent. Check it out here - [gstack](https://github.com/garrytan/gstack).

gstack has been a hot topic the last few days on X.

The opinion ranges from:
<Tweet url='https://x.com/neogoose_btw/status/2035071601008644242' />

To:
<Tweet url="https://x.com/Toby_Elk/status/2035237109393760752" />

As with everything else in the world, the truth lies somewhere in between. The value lies in Garry's years of experience coded into skills, however you perceive it.

I disagree with many of Garry's takes. I disagree with how gstack is positioned. But I also find gstack valuable.

And he's not selling it for thousands of dollars, which he could. It's open source.

Try it out. Or maybe don't. We could do with less negativity though.
